Commercial Analytics:
Customer Behavior on a Website


Website design influences consumer behavior and brand reputation. A polished and user-friendly website encourages users to remain on the website, send links to friends, and purchase products. Obtaining a deeper understanding of consumer behavior is imperitive for business' to design websites that mold potential visitors into actual customers. One method of delving into this commercial questions is through the behavior of website visitors, which reveal trends about the purchasing potential of the consumer.

The website behavior of approximately 455K users, who predominantly live in London, on an unspecified webpage is detailed in a dataset on Kaggle. It provides a glipse into the influence of technology in the private sector - a booming area intersection of fields. Amazon, an e-commerce website, recieves 2.5M visitors, who live in the United States, a month. Those visitors may have accounts on the site or be new to the site and these actions influence the purchasing potential of these consumers. Very similar to a show window, some of those visitors may just virtually window shop, while others may actually purchase products on the site. Honing into the behaviors of these consumers provides valuable insight into the interactions of people with webpages.

On average, a website visitor in the dataset conducts 3 activities on the webpage. The acitives may include adding to a wishlist, signing into an account, investigating the size of products. The more streamlined the process, then the less effort on the visitor to purchase a product. For example, a returning visitor may not be required to sign-into their account on every visit, which is one less step they need to take before purchasing a product on the website.

The dataset under invesitgation contains more vistitors who did not purchase a product, than visitors who did purchase a product, which is important to note, when trying to delve into the purchasing potential of a customer. Generally the customers visiting the website tended to visit on their mobile device. This is important to note because the user experience must be seemlessly designed for all devices, with a priortization on mobile devices.

The visitors in the dataset are approximately evenly split between returning and new users emphasizing that the website manages to balance both maintaining a steady flow of new users and repeat users. The website is both expanding its potential consumer base as well as mainting a base of loyal visitors, demonstatrating a design that is effective in a attracting new visitors and maintaining current clients.
