Commercial Analytics:
Customer Behavior on a Website


Website design influences consumer behavior and brand reputation. A polished and user-friendly website encourages users to remain on the website, send links to friends, and purchase products. Obtaining a deeper understanding of consumer behavior is imperitive for business' to design websites that mold potential visitors into actual customers. One method of delving into this commercial questions is through the behavior of website visitors, which reveal trends about the purchasing potential of the consumer.

The website behavior of approximately 455K users on an unspecified webpage is detailed in a dataset on Kaggle. It provides a glipse into the influence of technology in the private sector - a booming area intersection of fields. Amazon, an e-commerce website, recieves 2.5M visitors, who live in the United States, a month. Those visitors may have accounts on the site or be new to the site and these actions influence the purchasing potential of these consumers. Very similar to a show window, some of those visitors may just virtually window shop, while others may actually purchase products on the site. Honing into the behaviors of these consumers provides valuable insight into the interactions of people with webpages.
